# Candidate A

According to the IETF's records, **RFC 9110 (HTTP Semantics)** was published in June 2022 as a Standards-Track document ([www.ietf.org](https://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc9110.html#:~:text=Category%3A%20Standards%20Track%20Published%3A%20June,2022)). It now carries STD 97 and is listed by the RFC Editor as an "Internet Standard" ([www.rfc-editor.org](https://www.rfc-editor.org/info/rfc9110/#:~:text=Internet%20Standard)) ([www.ietf.org](https://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc9110.html#:~:text=STD%3A%2097%20Obsoletes%3A)). In other words, it is intended to be the definitive HTTP semantics specification. Its own abstract says that it "describes the overall architecture of HTTP, establishes common terminology, and defines aspects of the protocol that are shared by all versions" ([www.ietf.org](https://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc9110.html#:~:text=describes%20the%20overall%20architecture%20of,%C2%B6)), and that it "updates RFC 3864 and obsoletes RFCs 2818, 7231, 7232, 7233, 7235, 7538, 7615, 7694, and portions of 7230" ([www.ietf.org](https://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc9110.html#:~:text=This%20document%20updates%20RFC%203864,%C2%B6)). This means RFC 9110's role is to consolidate and replace the older fragmented HTTP/1.x specifications with a single core semantics document. We have found no indication of any newer RFC superseding it or of any change in its status since publication. (Our summary is based on the cited IETF/RFC Editor sources; if the IETF standards process were to reclassify or update RFC 9110, that would only appear in later official records.)
